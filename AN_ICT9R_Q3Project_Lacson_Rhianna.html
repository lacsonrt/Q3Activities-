Fire Safety Awareness

Fire safety awareness

defined as the management practices used to stop fires from starting, the controls that regulate fire systems during crises, and the fire safety training suppression techniques needed to put out a fire if it gets out of hand.


An attitude toward fire safety is one that involves knowledge of four fundamental areas, which are:


  • The Nature of Fire
  • The Causes of Fire
  • The Behavior of Fire
  • Fire Safety Management

Hence, three crucial components make up fire safety management:


  • Fire Prevention
  • Fire Protection
  • Fire Suppression






Fire Prevention

Fire Prevention

Aiming to avoid and put out destructive fires, these measures and techniques.


The aim of fire prevention is to inform the public and workers about adopting proactive measures to put out fires before they start and to lessen their destructive effects.

Electricity

Electricity

When utilized improperly, electrical distribution equipment creates major fire safety risks, some of which are even lethal.


Electrical Safety

  • If there are young children in the house, cover electrical outlets with plastic safety covers.
  • All electrical cords that are frayed, loose, or otherwise damaged should be replaced or repaired.
  • Recall that signs of probable wiring issues include dimming or flickering household lights, a TV picture that gets smaller, frequent fuse blowouts, or often tripping circuit breakers.

Heating

Heating

The second-leading cause of home fires annually and the primary cause of house fires in the winter is heating equipment. The many types of heating apparatus include fireplaces, wood burners, portable space heaters, and permanent space heaters. Portable space heaters are involved in nearly half of all fatalities linked to fires involving home heating equipment.p>

Heating Safety

  • Have a competent specialist inspect your home's heating system every year.
  • Ensure that any gas and wood-burning heaters are vented to the building's exterior.
  • If your home has gas or wood-burning appliances, think about placing a carbon monoxide alarm in a prominent area outside of the each bedroom.
